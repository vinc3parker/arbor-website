We've been building Aegle for a while now, and today we're opening it up to real humans for the first time. If you're the kind of person who tracks their training, cares about performance, and enjoys being an early adopter (read: willing to tell us when something breaks), this is for you.

Aegle is Arbor's athletic development app — built for hybrid athletes, recreational runners, and anyone who takes their training seriously but doesn't want to juggle five different apps to do it. Log sessions, track performance, and get smarter about how you train over time.

We're not calling this a full launch. We're calling it a beta, because that's what it is — a real, working app that we want real people using, so we can learn what's working, what isn't, and what we haven't thought of yet.

---

## How to get it on your phone

It takes about two minutes. Here's how:

**Step 1 — Download TestFlight**

TestFlight is Apple's official app for beta testing. If you don't have it, grab it from the App Store first.

[Download TestFlight](https://apps.apple.com/app/testflight/id899247664)

**Step 2 — Join the Aegle Beta**

Tap the link below on your iPhone. It'll open TestFlight and show you the option to install Aegle.

[Join the Aegle Beta →](https://testflight.apple.com/join/U6JcFCB7)

**Step 3 — Install and open Aegle**

Hit install, open the app, and create your account. The whole thing takes under a minute.

That's it. No waitlist, no invite code. Just tap and go.

## What we're looking for

We want to know what it actually feels like to use Aegle — not just whether it crashes (though please tell us if it does).

Does the onboarding make sense? Is the training data you care about there? Does anything feel clunky or confusing? Is there something obviously missing?

You can leave feedback directly inside TestFlight, email us at vinc3.parker@gmail.com, or join the [Aegle Beta WhatsApp group](https://chat.whatsapp.com/KkBT5ylacnR3qZwkPVAOjI?s=cl&p=i&ilr=2) where we'll share updates and you can drop thoughts as you go. We read everything.
